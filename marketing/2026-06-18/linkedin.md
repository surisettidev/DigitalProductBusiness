I audited my freelancer SaaS spend last week.

₹4,380/month going out the door. Across nine tools.

When I cut the overlap, I landed at ₹1,650/month for the same output. Three of the tools I was paying for had free tiers that covered everything I actually used. Two were "I'll cancel next month" survivors from 2024.

This is normal. Most Indian freelancers I talk to are paying ₹2,000–₹4,000/month for shelf-ware.

The fix takes 30 minutes:

1. Open your last bank statement
2. List every recurring charge tagged as a tool or SaaS
3. Next to each one, write: *the last time I opened this in the last 30 days*
4. Cancel anything you can't remember opening twice

The painful one for me: a project management tool I'd been paying ₹600/month for since 2024 because "I'll set it up properly soon." I had four cards in it. Total. Cancelled, moved to a free Notion page, lost zero workflow.

The honest tool stack you need in India in 2026 is smaller than the internet wants you to think. Invoicing + payment receiving + one project surface + one writing AI + one chat AI. That's it. Five tools. The rest is optional.

I'm putting together a quarterly-refreshed guide on this — what to pay for, what to drop, India-specific. Waitlist on the site if you want first access when it ships.
