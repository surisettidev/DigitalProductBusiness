## Section 1 — Invoicing & GST

### The problem you're solving
You need to send invoices that:
1. Are GST-compliant (HSN/SAC code, your GSTIN if registered, client GSTIN if B2B, place of supply)
2. Look professional enough that a CFO at a 200-person company will pay them
3. Generate recurring invoices for retainers without you re-typing
4. Export to a format your CA can read at year-end

### The tools

**Refrens** — invoicing + lead CRM, India-built
- Free tier: 20 invoices/month, GST-compliant, recurring invoices, multi-currency
- Paid tier: ₹449/month — unlimited invoices, custom domain emails, e-signatures
- India notes: GSTIN field is mandatory by default. HSN/SAC dropdown is pre-populated. Payment collection via Razorpay/Cashfree built in.
- **Verdict: Use.** Best free tier for Indian freelancers in 2026.

**Zoho Invoice** — free forever, full GST suite
- Free tier: Unlimited invoices, GST returns export, time tracking, 1 user
- Paid tier: N/A (Zoho Invoice itself is free; Zoho Books at ₹749/month for accounting)
- India notes: Truly free, Indian company, GST-native. Slightly heavier UI than Refrens.
- **Verdict: Use** if you want one tool to last you for years without ever paying.

**Vyapar** — desktop-first, popular with shop-style freelancers (videographers, photographers)
- Free tier: Mobile app free, desktop trial 15 days
- Paid tier: ₹3,599/year (desktop)
- India notes: Strong if you do offline + cash invoices. Overkill for digital freelancers.
- **Verdict: Skip** (digital freelancer) / **Use** (you also do offline work).

**Sleek Bill** — simple invoicing, India-focused
- Free tier: 20 invoices/month
- Paid tier: ₹599/year (annual!) — unlimited
- India notes: Cheapest paid plan in the category. UI is dated but works.
- **Verdict: Use if** Refrens free tier is too restrictive and you don't want a subscription.

**indinero, FreshBooks, Wave** — international tools
- **Verdict: Skip.** None handle Indian GST natively. You'll spend more time fighting the tool than invoicing.

### What I'd actually use
- **Just starting (0-10 invoices/month):** Zoho Invoice (free, forever)
- **Growing (10-50 invoices/month):** Refrens free tier + Razorpay payment links pasted in
- **Past ₹20L turnover:** Zoho Books (₹749/mo) — you need full books, not just invoices

### GST quick reference
- GST registration **mandatory** above ₹20 lakh turnover (₹10 lakh for special-category states)
- Below threshold? You can voluntarily register, but only if your clients are B2B and want to claim ITC. Otherwise it's pure paperwork.
- Place of supply rules: client in same state = CGST + SGST. Different state = IGST. International = export of services, zero-rated, but still LUT bond required.
- HSN/SAC code for most freelance services: **9983** (other professional services) or **998361** (advertising) or **998314** (IT consulting). When unsure, 9983 is safe.

## Section 2 — Receiving payments

### 2.1 Domestic (INR)

**UPI (direct bank account)**
- Free tier: ₹0 fee, instant
- Paid tier: N/A
- India notes: Best for small invoices (₹5K-₹50K) with trusted clients. No paper trail beyond bank statement. Don't use for B2B above ₹50K — clients want a tax invoice.
- **Verdict: Use** for small, fast collections from individuals.

**Razorpay**
- Free tier: ₹0 setup, ₹0 monthly. Per-transaction: 2% UPI (capped ₹0 above ₹2,000 for India domestic UPI), 2% cards, 2% netbanking + 18% GST on fees.
- Paid tier: Custom enterprise rates above ₹10L/month volume.
- India notes: Best dashboard, best developer docs, best webhook reliability. Payouts T+2 standard, T+1 with Razorpay X. Payment Links (no website needed) make this useful even for non-tech freelancers.
- **Verdict: Use.** Default for Indian digital freelancers.

**Cashfree Payments**
- Free tier: Similar pricing to Razorpay
- Paid tier: T+1 payout by default
- India notes: Cheaper FX on international cards (~3.5% vs Razorpay's 3%+). Better for SaaS-style recurring.
- **Verdict: Use** if you do USD cards + want T+1 default.

**Instamojo**
- Free tier: 2% + ₹3 per transaction (UPI), 5% on international cards
- Paid tier: ₹4,999/year removes platform fee on UPI
- India notes: Easiest "no website needed" payment link. Slower payouts (T+3). Cards FX is expensive.
- **Verdict: Skip** unless you want Instamojo's "store" feature for digital downloads.

### 2.2 International (USD/EUR/GBP)

**Wise (formerly TransferWise)**
- Free tier: ₹0 monthly. FX fees: 0.4-0.6% over mid-market rate. Receive USD/EUR/GBP via local account details.
- Paid tier: N/A
- India notes: Cheapest legit way to receive international payments. Gives you US/UK/EU bank account numbers — your clients pay locally, you receive INR in India. FIRC (Foreign Inward Remittance Certificate) available on request — needed for GST LUT compliance.
- **Verdict: Use.** Default for any freelancer with non-Indian clients.

**Payoneer**
- Free tier: ₹0, but $29.95/year if no transaction in 12 months
- Paid tier: 1-3% per withdrawal to Indian bank, plus FX spread (~2%)
- India notes: Easier integration with Upwork/Fiverr (they pay Payoneer directly). FIRC available. Slightly more expensive than Wise but legit.
- **Verdict: Use** if your platform forces it (Fiverr, some Upwork legacy). Otherwise Wise wins.

**Stripe (via Stripe Atlas + US LLC)**
- Free tier: 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction (US Stripe)
- Paid tier: Stripe Atlas costs $500 to set up a US LLC
- India notes: Only makes sense if you're earning ₹2L+/month from US clients AND want to look like a US company AND can handle US tax filing. For 95% of Indian freelancers, this is overkill.
- **Verdict: Skip** unless you specifically need a US bank account for SaaS billing or YC-style fundraising.

**Deel**
- Free tier: For freelancers, free to use. Deel charges the client.
- Paid tier: N/A (freelancer side)
- India notes: If a US/EU client says "we use Deel for contractors," accept. They handle compliance, you get clean payouts. Otherwise no reason to onboard yourself.
- **Verdict: Use** only when client requires it.

**PayPal**
- Free tier: 4.4% + fixed fee per transaction for international, plus 3-4% FX spread baked in
- India notes: Expensive. Holds funds randomly. Still useful for clients in regions Wise doesn't cover well (parts of South America, parts of Africa).
- **Verdict: Skip** if Wise works for that corridor. Use as last resort.

### What I'd actually use
- **Indian clients only:** Razorpay Payment Links + UPI for friends/small invoices
- **Mixed Indian + Western clients:** Razorpay + Wise (this is the default 2026 stack)
- **Upwork/Fiverr-heavy:** Payoneer (forced) + Wise (for direct clients you peel off the platform)
- **Heavy US client base, ₹3L+/month:** consider Stripe Atlas in Year 2, not Year 1

### Hidden cost most freelancers miss
On a $1,000 invoice paid through PayPal: you net ~₹78,500
On the same $1,000 paid through Wise: you net ~₹83,000
**That's ₹4,500 lost on a single invoice.** Across a year of 12 invoices: ₹54,000.

## Section 4 — Contracts (with Indian Stamp Act clauses)

### The problem you're solving
A written contract is the difference between getting paid and chasing payment for 6 months. Indian Stamp Act requires certain agreements to be stamped to be admissible in court — most freelance digital service agreements don't need stamping but should be e-signed for evidentiary weight.

### The tools

**Docusign**
- Free tier: 3 envelopes/month (Personal)
- Paid tier: ₹855/month (Personal) / ₹2,250/month (Standard)
- India notes: International standard. Indian courts accept e-signatures under IT Act 2000 Section 5. Slightly expensive.
- **Verdict: Use** if dealing with US/EU clients who insist.

**Leegality**
- Free tier: 10 documents/month
- Paid tier: ₹1,499/month
- India notes: India-built, Aadhaar e-sign integrated (strongest evidentiary value in Indian courts), stamp paper integration for ₹100-₹500 stamp duty.
- **Verdict: Use** for B2B Indian clients above ₹1L contract value.

**Eversign / Dropbox Sign (formerly HelloSign)**
- Free tier: 3 signature requests/month
- Paid tier: ₹1,250/month
- India notes: Solid international option, decent India support.
- **Verdict: Use** if Docusign feels overkill and Leegality feels too India-heavy.

**Plain PDF + scanned signature**
- Free tier: Free
- India notes: Less evidentiary weight than Aadhaar e-sign, but for retainer-style ongoing relationships with established clients, this is fine.
- **Verdict: Use** for invoices below ₹50K with repeat clients.

### What I'd actually use
- **Indian B2B client, contract value ₹1L+:** Leegality with Aadhaar e-sign
- **Mixed clients, occasional contracts:** Docusign personal plan
- **Repeat clients you trust:** PDF + scanned signature (don't waste subscription money)

### Indian Stamp Act quick notes
- Stamp duty on freelance service contracts: typically ₹100-₹500 depending on state (varies; check your state's rate)
- Service contracts under ₹50,000 value: stamp duty is usually nominal or zero
- Above ₹1L value, B2B: stamp duty becomes meaningful — Leegality handles this automatically
- E-signed digital contracts are **legally valid in India** under the IT Act 2000

### Bare-minimum contract clauses (every freelance contract should have these)
1. Scope (in plain English — what you're doing, what you're not)
2. Deliverables list with dates
3. Payment schedule (typically 40-50% upfront for new clients)
4. Revision limits (e.g., "2 rounds of revisions included; additional rounds at ₹X each")
5. Out-of-scope clause ("Anything not listed above is additional, quoted separately")
6. Termination clause (30 days notice, kill fee = 50% of remaining contract)
7. IP ownership ("IP transfers to client on full payment, not before")
8. Confidentiality (both ways)
9. Governing law (your state, your city)
10. Late payment penalty (2% per month is industry standard in India)

## Section 5 — Time tracking + automated invoicing

### The problem you're solving
If you bill hourly: you need accurate time. If you bill fixed-fee: you still need to track time to see your effective hourly rate per project — that's how you know which clients to keep and which to fire.

### The tools

- Free tier: Unlimited tracking, 5 users, basic reports
- Paid tier: ₹670/month ($8) per user
- India notes: Best UX. One-click start, browser extension, desktop app, mobile app. Tagging system is excellent.
- **Verdict: Use.** Default for time tracking.

**Clockify**
- Free tier: Unlimited everything except some advanced reports
- Paid tier: ₹250-₹830/month ($3-$10) per user
- India notes: Best free tier of any time tracker. Slightly less polished UI than Toggl.
- **Verdict: Use** if Toggl free tier limits hit you.

**Harvest**
- Free tier: 1 user, 2 projects
- Paid tier: ₹1,020/month ($12) per user
- India notes: Time tracking + invoicing in one tool. Good integration with Stripe/PayPal but weak with Razorpay.
- **Verdict: Skip** for India — use Toggl + Refrens combo instead.

**RescueTime**
- Free tier: Basic auto-tracking
- Paid tier: ₹500/month ($6) — full features
- India notes: Auto-tracks what apps/sites you use. Reveals where time actually goes. Different category from billable tracking.
- **Verdict: Use** as a self-discipline tool, not as a client-billing tool.

### What I'd actually use
- **Pure billable hours focus:** Toggl Track free tier
- **Cost-sensitive, want everything free:** Clockify free tier

### The ₹500/hour insight
Track every project's hours for 30 days. Calculate your effective hourly rate per client. You will discover that one client pays you ₹2,500/hour and another pays you ₹300/hour for "the same work." Fire the ₹300/hour client. **This single insight is worth more than the entire stack.**

## Appendix — GST-compliant invoice walkthrough

A GST-compliant invoice must have **all** of these. Missing any one and your client cannot claim ITC, and you may face penalties at audit.

### Mandatory fields
1. **Supplier name + address + GSTIN** (if registered) — top of invoice
2. **Invoice number** — unique, sequential, year-prefixed (e.g., `2026-27/INV/001`)
3. **Invoice date** — DD-MM-YYYY
4. **Customer name + address + GSTIN** (if B2B)
5. **Place of supply** — state name. Determines CGST+SGST vs IGST.
6. **HSN/SAC code** for each service line
7. **Description of service** — plain English
8. **Quantity / unit** (for services, usually "1" or "1 hour")
9. **Rate per unit**
10. **Total taxable value**
11. **GST rate (18% for most freelance services)**
12. **CGST + SGST amounts** (same state) OR **IGST amount** (different state) OR **0% with LUT reference** (export)
13. **Total invoice value in numbers + words**
14. **Signature** (digital or wet)
15. **Bank account details** for payment

### A copy-paste invoice template will be provided as a separate Word + PDF + Google Doc file along with this guide.

### Common mistakes
- **Missing place of supply:** invoice rejected at GST audit
- **Wrong tax type (CGST+SGST when client is in another state):** client cannot claim ITC; you'll redo the invoice under deadline pressure
- **Non-sequential invoice numbers:** red flag at audit, suggests skipped invoices
- **HSN code missing:** mandatory for turnover above ₹5 crore, recommended for all
- **For export of services:** must mention "Export under LUT — IGST not charged" + your LUT reference number

### When to register for GST
- Mandatory: turnover > ₹20 lakh/year (₹10 lakh in special-category states)
- Voluntary makes sense if: your clients are B2B and want to claim ITC (your invoice value goes UP by 18%, but they get it back)
- Voluntary makes no sense if: your clients are individuals / B2C — you just lose 18% pricing power
