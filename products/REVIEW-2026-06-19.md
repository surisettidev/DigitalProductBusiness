## Why the new "Future editions" language matters

The old "Quarterly update guarantee" was a **push-style** promise: "I will email you the new version every [period] for 2 years from purchase". This has three failure modes:

1. **Unsubscribe hygiene risk** — once you have an email, you're locked into sending whether the buyer still wants it or not. Indian DPDP Act 2023 makes this an explicit consent issue.
2. **Promised-against-unknown-demand** — if a product only sells 10 copies, the maintenance cost per buyer is huge. The promise doesn't account for that.
3. **No incentive to leave** — buyers never have a reason to come back to the site, so conversion-to-other-products suffers.

The new "Future editions — how to get them" is **pull-style**: the buyer emails when they want the next edition. This:

1. **Active-demand filter** — buyers who email are buyers who still care (highest-LTV signal in the data).
2. **Allows charging for future editions** if the product doesn't sustain demand (always told before any charge).
3. **Lets the product retire gracefully** — buyers can be migrated to whatever the closest current equivalent is.
4. **Shifts the burden of time-sensitive facts to the buyer** — the doc says "this is a starting point, not a live monitoring service" so the buyer verifies tax rules / tool pricing themselves.

The downside is more email-handling work for the owner. The mitigation is the 7-business-day reply SLA being stated up front.
